# Quality Control Testing Protocols for Cleaning Supplies Manufacturing

This document outlines the comprehensive Quality Control (QC) testing protocols for all 6 products manufactured by cleaning-supplies-manufacturing. These protocols ensure that all products meet the highest standards of quality, safety, and efficacy before release.

## 1. Visual Inspection Criteria

### General Criteria (Applicable to All 6 Products):
*   **Packaging Integrity:** Check for leaks, dents, cracks, or damage to bottles, caps, and seals.
*   **Labeling Accuracy:** Verify correct product name, batch number, manufacturing/expiration dates, ingredients, usage instructions, and safety warnings.
*   **Fill Volume/Weight:** Ensure products are filled to the specified volume or weight, visually inspecting for under-fills or over-fills.
*   **Appearance (Liquid/Gel Products):** Inspect for proper color, clarity (absence of turbidity or foreign particles), consistency, and absence of separation.
*   **Appearance (Solid Products - e.g., wipes, powders):** Check for uniform size, shape, color, and absence of discoloration, clumping, or foreign material.

### Product-Specific Visuals:
*   **Product 1 (e.g., All-Purpose Cleaner):** Note uniform liquid consistency, clear appearance, and absence of precipitates.
*   **Product 2 (e.g., Disinfectant Spray):** Verify fine mist spray pattern, no clogging in nozzle, and clear solution.
*   **Product 3 (e.g., Glass Cleaner):** Ensure streak-free clarity, consistent blue tint (if applicable), and no residues.
*   **Product 4 (e.g., Floor Cleaner):** Check for proper viscosity, homogeneous solution, and absence of suspended solids.
*   **Product 5 (e.g., Bathroom Cleaner):** Observe foamy consistency (if applicable), uniform color, and no abrasive particles.
*   **Product 6 (e.g., Hand Soap):** Verify pearlized appearance (if applicable), proper viscosity, and consistent fragrance.

## 2. Chemical Tests

### General Criteria (Applicable to All 6 Products):
*   **pH Measurement:** Verify pH within specified range for each product to ensure safety and efficacy.
*   **Active Ingredient Concentration:** Test to confirm active ingredients (e.g., surfactants, disinfectants, acids) are within their specified concentration ranges.
*   **Specific Gravity/Density:** Measure to confirm product consistency and proper formulation.
*   **Viscosity:** For liquid/gel products, measure viscosity to ensure proper flow and consistency.

### Product-Specific Chemical Tests:
*   **Product 1 (All-Purpose Cleaner):** Surfactant concentration, alkalinity.
*   **Product 2 (Disinfectant Spray):** Quaternary Ammonium Compound (QAC) concentration, alcohol content (if applicable).
*   **Product 3 (Glass Cleaner):** Volatile Organic Compound (VOC) content, ammonia content (if applicable).
*   **Product 4 (Floor Cleaner):** Detergency test, non-volatile matter.
*   **Product 5 (Bathroom Cleaner):** Acid concentration (if applicable), chelating agent concentration.
*   **Product 6 (Hand Soap):** Total fatty matter, moisture content, preservative efficacy.

## 3. Stability Tests

### General Criteria (Applicable to All 6 Products):
*   **Accelerated Stability (Heat/Cold Cycle):** Store samples at elevated temperatures (e.g., 40°C) and low temperatures (e.g., 5°C) for defined periods to simulate aging and observe for physical and chemical changes (e.g., separation, color change, odor change, precipitation).
*   **Long-Term Stability (Real-Time):** Store samples under ambient conditions (e.g., 25°C) for the duration of the product's shelf life, periodically checking all visual and chemical parameters.
*   **Freeze-Thaw Stability:** Subject samples to multiple freeze-thaw cycles (e.g., -5°C to 25°C) to assess product integrity under temperature fluctuations.
*   **Light Stability:** Expose samples to intense light for a specified period to check for photodegradation.

## 4. Efficacy Tests

### General Criteria (Applicable to All 6 Products, where relevant):
*   **Cleaning Performance:** Test against standardized soil types (e.g., grease, oil, dust) on various surfaces to evaluate cleaning power.
*   **Disinfection/Sanitization Efficacy (for relevant products):** Conduct microbiological tests (e.g., AOAC Use-Dilution Test, ASTM E2315) against target microorganisms (bacteria, viruses) at specified contact times.
*   **Odor Removal:** Assess the ability to neutralize or eliminate odors.
*   **Streak/Residue Test:** For surface cleaners, evaluate for any streaks or residues left after drying.

## 5. Safety Tests

### General Criteria (Applicable to All 6 Products):
*   **Skin Irritation/Sensitization:** Conduct patch tests or in vitro assays to assess potential for skin irritation or allergic reactions.
*   **Eye Irritation:** In vitro assays to evaluate potential for eye irritation.
*   **Oral Toxicity:** In vitro assays or literature review to assess potential for oral toxicity.
*   **Flammability:** For products containing flammable ingredients, test flammability according to relevant standards.
*   **Heavy Metals Screening:** Test for the absence or presence below regulated limits of heavy metals (e.g., Lead, Arsenic, Mercury).

## 6. Equipment Requirements

*   **pH Meter:** Calibrated regularly, with appropriate electrodes for different product types.
*   **Viscometer:** For measuring liquid/gel product viscosity.
*   **Spectrophotometer/Chromatography Equipment:** For active ingredient analysis.
*   **Incubators/Environmental Chambers:** For stability testing (heat, cold, humidity control).
*   **Autoclave/Sterilization Equipment:** For microbiological efficacy testing.
*   **Microscope:** For visual inspection of microbial growth in efficacy tests.
*   **Analytical Balances:** For precise weighing of samples and reagents.
*   **Refractometer:** For measuring refractive index of certain liquid products.
*   **Specific Gravity Hydrometers/Pycnometers:** For density measurements.
*   **Lab Glassware:** Beakers, flasks, pipettes, burettes, etc.

## 7. Calibration Schedules

*   **Daily:** pH meters (before each use or daily checks with buffer solutions).
*   **Weekly:** Analytical balances (with certified weights), refractometers.
*   **Monthly:** Viscometers, spectrophotometers.
*   **Quarterly:** Environmental chambers, incubators, specific gravity equipment.
*   **Annually:** All major analytical equipment requiring professional calibration.
*   **Documentation:** All calibration records to be maintained, including dates, results, and next due date.

## 8. Record Keeping Requirements

*   **Batch Records:** Detailed documentation for each product batch, including raw material lot numbers, manufacturing parameters, and in-process QC results.
*   **QC Test Reports:** Comprehensive reports for all finished product QC tests, including test methods, results, acceptance criteria, and analyst signatures.
*   **Stability Study Data:** Raw data, observations, and conclusions from all stability studies.
*   **Calibration Logs:** Records for all calibrated equipment.
*   **Deviation and OOS Reports:** Documentation of any deviations from standard procedures or Out-of-Specification (OOS) results, including investigations and corrective actions.
*   **Retention Samples:** Retain samples from each batch for the duration of its shelf life plus one year.

## 9. Failure Investigation Procedures

1.  **Identification of Failure:** Immediately flag any OOS or deviation from established QC parameters.
2.  **Quarantine:** Isolate the affected batch (or raw material) to prevent further processing or shipment.
3.  **Initial Assessment:** Review batch records, QC test reports, and raw material COAs to identify potential immediate causes.
4.  **Re-testing:** Perform re-testing of the sample by a different analyst using calibrated equipment. If re-test confirms OOS, proceed to full investigation.
5.  **Root Cause Analysis (RCA):** Conduct a thorough investigation using tools like 5 Whys, Fishbone diagrams, etc., to identify the fundamental cause of the failure. This may involve:
    *   Reviewing manufacturing process parameters.
    *   Testing raw materials.
    *   Evaluating equipment performance.
    *   Assessing personnel training and procedures.
6.  **Corrective and Preventative Actions (CAPA):** Develop and implement actions to correct the current failure and prevent recurrence. This could include process adjustments, equipment repair, raw material supplier changes, or updated training.
7.  **Documentation:** Document all aspects of the investigation, including findings, RCA, CAPA, and verification of CAPA effectiveness.
8.  **Disposition:** Based on the investigation, determine the disposition of the affected batch (e.g., rework, scrap, release).

These protocols apply to all products (Product 1 through Product 6) and are designed to ensure consistent quality and compliance with all relevant regulations.
