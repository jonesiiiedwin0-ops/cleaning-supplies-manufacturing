# Safety Checklist

## Pre-Shift Safety Briefing Checklist
- [ ] Review daily tasks and associated hazards
- [ ] Discuss any new safety concerns or updates
- [ ] Confirm understanding of emergency procedures

## PPE Inspection Checklist
- [ ] Hard hats: No cracks, dents, or damage
- [ ] Safety glasses: Clean, scratch-free, and proper fit
- [ ] Gloves: Appropriate for task, no tears or holes
- [ ] Safety footwear: Good condition, slip-resistant soles
- [ ] Hearing protection: Clean and functional

## Hazmat Handling Checklist
- [ ] SDS accessible and reviewed
- [ ] Proper labeling on all containers
- [ ] Use designated hazmat storage areas
- [ ] Spill kits readily available and stocked
- [ ] Follow all handling and disposal protocols

<h2>Ventilation Verification Checklist</h2>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Ensure all ventilation systems are operational</li>
<li>[ ] Verify adequate airflow in work areas</li>
<li>[ ] Check for any blockages or obstructions in vents</li>
</ul>

## Emergency Equipment Checklist
- [ ] Fire extinguishers: Charged and easily accessible
- [ ] First aid kits: Stocked and up-to-date
- [ ] Emergency eyewash/showers: Functional and clear access
- [ ] Emergency exits: Unobstructed and clearly marked

## Incident Response Checklist
- [ ] Secure the area and prevent further harm
- [ ] Provide immediate first aid if necessary
- [ ] Report incident to supervisor
- [ ] Document all details of the incident
- [ ] Cooperate with investigation

<h2>End-of-Shift Safety Review</h2>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Review any safety incidents or near-misses from the shift</li>
<li>[ ] Ensure all equipment is powered off or secured</li>
<li>[ ] Confirm work area is clean and organized</li>
</ul>

## Weekly Safety Audit Checklist
- [ ] Inspect all machinery and tools for defects
- [ ] Review safety training records
- [ ] Conduct mock emergency drills
- [ ] Evaluate effectiveness of safety procedures
- [ ] Address any identified hazards or non-compliance
